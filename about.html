About

I'm Paul Sonderegger, and I started Kinch to help business leaders make better decisions about AI and data.

I've spent my career at the intersection of data strategy and business value—at companies where data wasn't a support function, it was the product. That experience gave me a clear view of what it actually takes to turn data into competitive advantage, and where most organizations go wrong.

What I've seen is that the companies that get the most from AI are the ones that get their data strategy right first. Not the ones with the biggest budgets or the most sophisticated technology—the ones with clear thinking about what data they have, what it's worth, and how to put it to work.

That's what Kinch is about: helping executives think clearly about AI and data so they can make confident, well-informed decisions.

Experience

  • Led data strategy at organizations where data is the core product
  • Advised executive teams on AI adoption and data-driven transformation
  • Built and scaled data capabilities from early stage through enterprise
  • Developed frameworks for evaluating AI investments and measuring outcomes

How I think about AI and data

Most companies approach AI backwards. They start with the technology and then go looking for problems to solve. The result is a lot of expensive experiments that never connect to the business.

I believe the right approach starts with data strategy. Understanding what data you have, what data you need, and how data creates value in your specific business. AI is a powerful tool, but only when it's pointed at the right problems with the right data behind it.
